# Problem 1

Proof:

> Assume there exists an $a \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $\forall a \in \mathbb{N}$ for all $b \in \mathbb{N}$.

> $a - 1 \in \mathbb{N}$, so it is not true that $a \in \mathbb{N}$ for all $b \in \mathbb{N}$. Therefore, the assumption is false. $\blacksquare$
